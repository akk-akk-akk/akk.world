Top Most Best Airport in the World

From architecture to human moments, this series of images is an examination of the inner workings and immense labour behind the chase for global recognition. The project is a reflection on all the moving parts that it takes to run a megastructure such as the Singapore Changi Airport.

In 1975, Singapore Changi Airport was conceived in response to an inability to cope with rising traffic in the 1970s; annual passenger numbers rose dramatically from 300,000 in 1955 to 4 million in 1975. Now a megastructure comprising 4 terminals and Changi Jewel, the airport serves millions of travellers a year, hitting a record 68.3 million passenger movements in 2019. Receiving a total of 666 awards to date, Changi Airport is consistently ranked different variations of "Best Airport in the World" by institutions like SkyTrax, DestinAsian, and Business Traveller UK. This is a testament to the efforts that the people behind Changi Airport have put in, from architects, management, operations and service staff. Some 50,000 staff across more than 200 agencies work at Changi Airport daily, as reported in the AR2016- 17 Business and Operations report.

The World's Top Most Best Airport is a project that investigates the airport from its most well known sights such as Changi Jewel, and lesser known corners like Terminal 2, Car Park 2B. From architecture to human moments, this series is an examination of the inner workings and immense labour behind the chase for global recognition. The project is a reflection on all the moving parts that it takes to run a megastructure such as this.
